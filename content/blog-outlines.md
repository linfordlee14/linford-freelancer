## 2. 5 Security Basics Every Small NGO Must Implement

**Target Audience:** Small nonprofits, remote teams, conservation organizations handling sensitive data

**Problem:** NGOs are increasingly targeted by phishing, ransomware, and data breaches but lack IT resources.

**Outline:**
1. **Introduction**
   - Why NGOs are targets (donor data, financial info, low defenses)
   - Real-world breach examples and consequences

2. **Security Basic #1: Strong Password Policies**
   - Use password managers (LastPass, 1Password, Bitwarden)
   - Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) everywhere

3. **Security Basic #2: Employee Training**
   - Phishing simulation campaigns
   - How to spot suspicious emails
   - Reporting procedures

4. **Security Basic #3: Regular Software Updates**
   - Why outdated software = open doors for hackers
   - Automate updates where possible

5. **Security Basic #4: Data Encryption & Backups**
   - Encrypt sensitive files (donor lists, financial records)
   - Cloud backup strategy (3-2-1 rule)

6. **Security Basic #5: Access Control**
   - Least privilege principle
   - Revoke access when staff leave

7. **Affordable Tools**
   - Free/low-cost security tools for small teams
   - When to hire a security consultant

8. **Call-to-Action**
   - Book a free security audit

## 3. Using AI Responsibly in Conservation: Lessons from RhinoGuardians

**Target Audience:** Conservation tech professionals, wildlife NGOs, AI ethics researchers

**Problem:** AI offers powerful conservation tools but raises questions about bias, privacy, and unintended consequences.

**Outline:**
1. **Introduction**
   - The promise of AI in conservation (detection, prediction, automation)
   - The responsibility that comes with deploying AI in sensitive contexts

2. **RhinoGuardians Project Overview**
   - Problem: Rhino poaching crisis
   - Solution: YOLOv5 AI detection system
   - Results: Real-time alerts, 95%+ accuracy

3. **Ethical Consideration #1: Data Privacy**
   - No human facial recognition to protect ranger/community privacy
   - Encrypted data transmission and storage
   - Consent from reserve managers

4. **Ethical Consideration #2: Avoiding Bias**
   - Training data diversity (multiple reserves, lighting conditions)
   - False positive/negative impacts (alert fatigue vs. missed threats)
   - Continuous model evaluation and retraining

5. **Ethical Consideration #3: Human-AI Collaboration**
   - AI as a tool, not a replacement for rangers
   - Importance of local knowledge and context
   - Transparent decision-making

6. **Ethical Consideration #4: Open Source & Accessibility**
   - Why we open-sourced RhinoGuardians
   - Making conservation tech accessible to under-resourced teams
   - Community contributions and improvements

7. **Lessons Learned**
   - What we'd do differently
   - Recommendations for other conservation AI projects

8. **Call-to-Action**
   - Invite collaboration, feedback, or consulting inquiries
